The Negative Effects of Global Warming

⠀

By: Breanna Lewis

Extinction caused by Global Warming

cc: Arctic-fox / tenor

As the Earth gets warmer and warmer, certain animals which relied on colder temperatures to survive are starting to become extinct. The warming of the planet has destroyed many of their homes and has devastated entire ecosystems. This extends not only to animals but to plants as well. A study found that by the year 2070 roughly one-third of all plant and animal species on the planet could be facing extinction. This would be extremely devastating to the food chain and global economy of the planet. Some towns and countries survive solely by exporting animal parts. Not only do animals and plants have to compete against the enviornment, but against humans as well. over-population has lead to humans needing to build more homes and buildings to accommodate for the excess population. Along with this, animals were already being over hunted.

How to Remedy the Extinction of Species

Unfortunately, there is no way to repair the damage that has already been done to the animals. What we can do is prevent further depopulation and spread of greenhouse gasses. If we start cutting down on greenhouse gasses now we can prevent further destruction to these ecosystems. For the animals we could also designate safe-havens for them. This would allow the animals to live and repopulate their species in peace without threat of their habitats being destroyed.
